2022

TOYOTA

[global navigation]

The ask: To enhance the digital navigation for Toyota and Lexus across their 40+ European markets, fix existing errors, and provide an intuitive and hassle-free way of discovering the world of Toyota

The challenge: To design an elevated navigation system that addresses various market requirements and consumer needs.

The ambition: To balance discoverability (the user's ability to find items) and eﬃciency (the user's ability to quickly find items without errors)

  • Information Architecture
  • Interaction Design
  • User Research and Testing
  • Cross-Market Strategy
  • Accessibility and Usability

Research Methodology

Our research combined insights from Toyota's internal teams, industry best practices, and user needs to ensure a balanced solution that considered all perspectives.

Toyota Perspective

We began the project with a collaborative workshop involving key market stakeholders to align on goals, surface constraints, and uncover opportunities. The session was structured to address both user experience and business needs.

This structured approach helped us:

  1. Identified localization constraints, such as language length and content variations across regions
  2. Explored UX triggers and structural considerations, including differences in behavior across desktop and mobile
  3. Defined measurable goals, setting performance metrics to track navigation success
  4. Mapped business opportunities, aligning potential features with broader strategic objectives
  5. Gathered likes and dislikes, learning from current pain points and successes across platforms
  6. Benchmarked references, analyzing best-in-class navigation models to inform our approach

Prioritization & Roadmapping

In a follow-up session, we focused on prioritizing the insights gathered from Workshop 1. Using the RICE framework (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) to objectively determine which should be tackled first—minimizing emotional or gut-based decision-making.

Together we:

  1. Estimate impact vs. effort across proposed features
  2. Align on short- and long-term priorities
  3. Lay the foundation for a clear and actionable roadmap

Revolution Approach

A top to bottom approach that looks at the larger system that needs to be resolved thereby requiring the redesign and testing of several components. The outcome is a refreshed navigation system.

Industry Perspective

We researched over 35 best-in-class sites across industry sectors and pulled the ones most relevant to DXP. Each site was looked at through a lens of five categories identified through earlier sessions that encompass the benchmarking elements.

Audience Perspective

We drew on consumer research and qualitative data to generate actionable insights into user behaviours, needs, and expectations.

Final Designs

After testing and validating several design directions via unmoderated online user sessions, we arrived at a final solution ready for handoff to development.

Content Guidelines

To ensure clarity and consistency across markets, we developed a set of navigation content guidelines. These covered tone of voice, labeling conventions, and hierarchy principles, ensuring users can quickly understand and interact with the navigation regardless of language or region.

Reflection

This was a significant change, requiring open communication with all markets, careful consideration of localization and language constraints, and ongoing feedback to understand what works, and what doesn't — in each region, resulting in a solution that's flexible yet true to the core strategy and information architecture.
